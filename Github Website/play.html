Technology and AI's Role in Prophecy & Daily Life

At its core, technology aims to enhance human efficiency. Its explosive growth into areas like artificial intelligence, brain-computer interfacing, and advanced data analytics facilitates capabilities that were unimaginable just decades ago. These innovations help predict consumer behaviors, automate industries, and even save lives.

But technology enters deeply spiritual waters when we consider systems like AI being tasked with replicating human intent, thought, and decision-making. Technology encroaches further when AI counselors surface in mental health domains or when chips interact directly with the human brain.

The ethical dilemma broadens when considering “targeted individuals” in the mental-health narrative (those alleging technological harassment). The mainstream medical community frequently attributes accounts of controlling technology or voices in the mind to conditions like schizophrenia. And yet, deeper explorations suggest that such experiences may resonate closer to biblical prophecy rather than technological manipulation.

The Ethical Fine Line

Innovations in brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) and cutting-edge AI might help paralyzed individuals gain agency over their environment. On one side, companies like Neuralink publicize technologies that create mobility where none exists. On the other, however, the cost is measured in millions of animal lives lost during experimentation.

This points to broader questions:

  • Are we aware of the true costs of technological advancement?
  • How do these intersect with acts of carelessness, or worse, deception under secular science disguised as compassion?
  • Have we forgotten that both humanity and animal life itself are interconnected spiritual beings?

Genesis 2 tells us that all creation—from humans to animals—is derived from the dust of the earth. To tread so heavily on living creation is to misinterpret the spiritual relationship between ourselves and God’s gifts.
